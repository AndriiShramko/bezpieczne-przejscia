Compliance: GDPR & AI Act — privacy by design

Our product thesis: identities are not retained, no register is kept, frames are not stored, faces and plates are pixelated, and law enforcement stays with the competent authority.

How compliance is designed in

  • Minimisation (GDPR Art. 5(1)(c)): only per-minute aggregate counters reach disk; the output data contains no personal data.
  • Pixelation of faces/plates at the analysis stage; the source frame exists only transiently in RAM during processing and is never persisted.
  • Zero biometrics: a face is only a region to pixelate — we never compute or store embeddings or biometric templates. The system performs no remote biometric identification (AI Act Art. 5).
  • Legal basis for the processing moment: legitimate interest (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f); purpose = road safety) — with an LIA and DPIA completed before any run on a real camera.
  • No automated decisions about individuals (Art. 22) — the output is infrastructure statistics, not an assessment of a person.
  • No register of offences (GDPR Art. 10) — the system attributes nothing to persons and keeps no lists.
  • Retention: aggregate counters carry no personal data; technical logs rotate automatically.

Role and status

The demonstrator is run by Andrii Shramko (sole trader, VAT PL7543116302) as data controller (demo). A production deployment for a public client runs on the authority's own legal basis (Art. 6(1)(e)) with a processing agreement (Art. 28) — the supplier then acts as processor.

What this system does NOT do

  • does not identify persons or vehicles, no face recognition;
  • does not impose penalties, nor support imposing them;
  • keeps no register of events attributed to persons;
  • does not stream or archive video.
