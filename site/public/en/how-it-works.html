How it works — and what we deliberately do NOT do

  1. Video source — a camera with the owner's written permission or our own camera. The failover pool (primary + backup) watches one and the same crossing.
  2. In-RAM analysis — detection (Apache-2.0-licensed model), pixelation of faces and plates right after detection, tracking with ephemeral IDs.
  3. Zone topology — line-crossing counters (pedestrians, vehicles), crosswalk occupancy, “driver failed to yield” derived topologically (transit of the conflict zone without stopping while a pedestrian occupies the crossing).
  4. Disk = counters only — stats_bucket + coverage_bucket (actually observed time) + camera_health. No frames, no embeddings.
  5. Dashboard — aggregate charts, coverage, failover status.

Honest limitations

  • % “head-down” is a sampled proxy indicator of attention with a wide confidence interval — NOT a claim that “the pedestrian used a phone”.
  • PET / TTC (conflict times in seconds) — only on a metrically calibrated camera. Without calibration we show unitless conflict events.
  • Panoramic framings (pedestrians a few pixels tall) → counting only; behavioural metrics switch off automatically.
  • Night, rain, snow reduce detection sensitivity — which is why every metric carries its actually-observed time (coverage).
  • Camera-based speed is a screening layer — never evidence. Enforcement requires certified state instruments.
